Overview

Predicting accidents before they happen on India's most dangerous urban corridors.

Safety Net is a concept exploration of an AI-powered, in-vehicle safety system that predicts and prevents road accidents in real time using deep learning and vehicle-to-vehicle (V2V) communication. The project focuses on high-risk urban highways such as Bengaluru's KIA Airport Road, where overspeeding, poor visibility, and dense traffic significantly increase accident probability.

01
Problem Statement

Why roads
still kill

India experiences one of the highest road fatality rates globally, with urban highways being especially dangerous due to high vehicle density, inconsistent enforcement, and challenging environmental conditions. Bengaluru's KIA Airport Road is a particularly high-risk corridor, recording frequent accidents caused by overspeeding, sudden braking, and limited visibility.

Current road safety systems — including speed cameras, signage, and in-vehicle assistance — are largely reactive, alerting drivers only after a dangerous situation arises. There is a critical need for a predictive, real-time warning system that anticipates risks before accidents occur, enabling proactive decision-making for drivers.

The system that saves a life can't wait until danger has already arrived.

02
Research

Data-driven
risk mapping

Secondary research using Bengaluru Traffic Police (2023) data identified severe crash concentrations along the Airport Road corridor, with Yelahanka Air Force Station (63 fatalities), Devanahalli Airport (58), and Chikkajala (39) emerging as the top three high-risk zones. Motorcyclists dominate fatality statistics at 59%, with 43% of deaths occurring between 6 PM—2 AM. Critical risk factors include overspeeding (37% on expressways), poor helmet compliance (34%), and hit-and-run incidents (30% of fatalities). These patterns reveal the need for predictive, location-specific interventions targeting high-speed segments and vulnerable road users rather than post-incident enforcement.

System Architecture & Data Flow

Real-time
processing pipeline

Real-Time Processing Pipeline

Sensor data, environmental signals, and nearby vehicle telemetry are continuously collected and pre-processed before entering the deep learning inference layer. The system computes a dynamic accident probability score in real time and triggers alerts only when defined risk thresholds are exceeded.

50m Contextual Inference Radius

Risk evaluation is restricted to a 50-meter radius to maintain contextual relevance and reduce signal noise. At highway speeds, this window provides sufficient reaction time (approximately 2—4 seconds) while maintaining computational efficiency.

Vehicle-to-Vehicle Communication

When elevated risk is detected, hazard and speed information are broadcast to nearby vehicles using V2V protocols. This transforms isolated risk detection into coordinated, network-aware accident prevention.

05
Risk Identification & Alert Logic

When to warn,
when to stay silent

Decision Threshold Control

The system does not trigger alerts continuously. Risk scores are computed in real time and evaluated against predefined confidence thresholds. Alerts are generated only when probability levels exceed calibrated limits, preventing unnecessary interruptions and reducing alert fatigue.

Accident-Prone Zone Alert

Triggered when a vehicle enters a historically high-risk corridor and contextual risk factors (speed variance, traffic density, weather conditions) elevate the probability score beyond the safety margin. This alert is location-aware and predictive rather than reactive.

Overspeeding Alert

Activated when vehicle speed exceeds adaptive limits determined by road type, surrounding vehicle velocity, and environmental conditions. Unlike static speed warnings, this alert considers behavioural deviation within the 50-meter inference radius.

When the System Does Not Alert

If risk remains below the calibrated threshold, or if contextual signals indicate stable driving behaviour, the system remains silent. This restraint ensures that alerts signal genuine risk rather than noise, preserving driver trust and usability.

06
Solution

From reactive
to predictive

01

Safety Net introduces a predictive, corridor-specific safety system designed for high-risk urban highways such as Bengaluru's KIA Airport Road. Rather than reacting to incidents, the system continuously analyses driving behaviour, environmental signals, and historical crash patterns to anticipate risk before it materialises.

02

By combining real-time telemetry with contextual intelligence, Safety Net enables vehicles to identify accident-prone zones, detect unsafe speed deviations, and respond proactively. Alerts are delivered through minimal, non-intrusive HUD and audio cues calibrated to avoid distraction while preserving driver attention.

03

The integration of Vehicle-to-Vehicle (V2V) communication transforms isolated vehicles into a shared safety network. When elevated risk is detected, hazard and speed information are broadcast within a defined radius, allowing nearby drivers to adjust behaviour collectively — particularly critical along fast-moving airport corridors.

04

Ultimately, Safety Net shifts road safety from reactive enforcement to predictive intervention, promoting smoother speed regulation, earlier deceleration in high-risk segments, and behaviour correction before accidents occur.

07
Challenges

Constraints &
honest limits

C.01
Infrastructure Dependency

Requires reliable environmental APIs, traffic density feeds, and V2V communication infrastructure along the Airport Road corridor.

C.02
Adoption Threshold

Cooperative risk-sharing improves with higher vehicle participation. Low adoption limits network-wide effectiveness.

C.03
Data Privacy & Security

Real-time telemetry and inter-vehicle communication require encryption, anonymisation, and regulatory compliance.

C.04
Sensor & Environmental Noise

Heavy rain, poor visibility, or inconsistent lane markings may reduce inference accuracy.

C.05
Model Calibration

Incorrect risk thresholds could lead to over-alerting or under-warning, affecting driver trust.
