Overview

From confusion to confidence — financial literacy that actually sticks.

Urushya is a gamified financial literacy mobile application designed to empower young adults (18–25) with practical money management skills. Through interactive modules, real-world simulations, mentor guidance, and smart budgeting tools, it transforms complex financial concepts into engaging, bite-sized experiences — bridging the gap between financial awareness and action.

Young adults in India lack easy access to simple, customised guidance on strategic finance management — creating a fear of even beginning. Financial illiteracy leads to poor decisions, financial stress, early loan dependency, and mounting debt.

Individuals in this age group yearn for safety, security, and confidence to take control of their future. Existing tools feel too overwhelming or untrustworthy, causing inconsistent use. There is an urgent need to build financial self-efficacy (FSE) — the belief that one can manage money effectively.

LEARN. APPLY. SECURE. — Guide young adults to make informed decisions and exercise confident control of their finances.

Challenges & Insights

What we
learned

C.01
Financial Jargon Overwhelm

Users felt paralysed by terms like "SIP," "compounding," and "credit score" — validated the need for visual, bite-sized, jargon-free learning.

C.02
Lack of Trust in Digital Tools

3 of 8 interviewees avoided finance apps due to privacy fears or past bad experiences — reinforced the need for transparent, mentor-backed guidance.

C.03
Passive Saving Habits

58% let money sit in savings accounts — highlighted urgency for active budgeting nudges and goal-setting features that drive behaviour change.

C.04
Desire for Real-World Practice

"I want to invest but don't know where to start" — directly led to the investment simulation feature planned for the next phase.

C.05
Gamification Validated

Users responded positively to reward systems in early lo-fi tests — confirmed strong engagement potential for badge and streak mechanics.

C.06
Mentor Demand — 100%

Every single interviewee wanted "someone to ask doubts" — solidified the mentor chat system as a non-negotiable core feature, not a nice-to-have.
