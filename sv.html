Overview

Smart cooking. Less waste. Community giving.

Savour is a behavioural UX project and meal-planning application designed to reduce household food waste through mindful consumption and efficient grocery organisation. It helps users track their food inventory, monitor expiry dates, and plan meals that make optimal use of available ingredients. With an interactive planner, smart grocery list, AI recipe generator, and donation hub, Savour turns everyday cooking into a more sustainable and community-oriented practice.

Food waste occurs when groceries are purchased but perishables are forgotten or left unused until they expire — or when users struggle to find practical ways to use leftover ingredients. Many individuals also find it difficult to donate surplus food efficiently.

Existing tools for managing food waste feel complex and time-consuming, causing users to revert to manual methods and unintentional waste. There is an urgent need for a solution that bridges awareness and action.

Final Design Screens

High-fidelity
in action

Built in FlutterFlow with smooth transitions, expiry-aware logic, and six core flows designed to reduce friction at every step of the sustainability journey.

Splash & Authentication

Calm onboarding that sets a purposeful tone. Sign up, log in, and personalisation questions collect dietary preferences and household size from the start.

Home & Navigation

A dashboard-first home surfaces expiry alerts, meal suggestions, and quick actions. Side drawer and notification panel keep everything reachable without clutter.

Meal Suggestions

AI recipe generator suggests meals from current pantry stock — prioritising ingredients closest to expiry. Shakshuka, Taktouka, Tomato Egg Curry and more. Save to favourites.

Pantry Management

Track ingredients with expiry dates, quantity levels, and smart filtering. Receipt scanning auto-populates stock, eliminating manual entry friction.

Sidebar Features

The sidebar extends Savour into a full sustainability toolkit — donation hub maps nearby food banks, meal planner schedules the week, and receipts log spending history.

Profile & Preferences

Edit dietary preferences, review waste and donation history, and access help — ensuring Savour adapts to changing habits and household needs over time.

Challenges & Insights

What we
learned

C.01
Prototype Validation Limits

At the conceptual stage, real-world validation was limited. Features like bill-based expiry detection needed further refinement and live testing to confirm accuracy.

C.02
AI Recipe Mapping Complexity

Mapping AI-generated recipes to exact pantry contents requires robust ingredient parsing. Early testing revealed edge cases in mixed-unit and regional ingredient naming.

C.03
Balancing Nudge & Guilt

Sustainability messaging had to feel supportive rather than guilt-driven. Ethical nudging — framing waste reduction as a positive achievement — proved essential to keep users engaged.

C.04
Donation UX Friction

Users were willing to donate but quickly lost motivation if the process required more than 2—3 steps. The donation hub had to be location-aware and near-instant to be adopted.

C.05
Manual Entry Resistance

Users consistently skipped manual pantry input during testing — validating receipt scanning as a non-negotiable feature rather than a nice-to-have.

C.06
Behavioural Design Complexity

Changing food habits is harder than changing interfaces. The project reinforced the importance of layering reminders, rewards, and social proof to drive lasting behaviour change.
